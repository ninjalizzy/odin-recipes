Authentic Louisiana Red Beans and Rice

Description

Louisiana-style red beans simmered nice and slow with Cajun spices develop into a rich gravy-like base, perfect for cool winter nights or any old Monday!

  • 1 lb. andouille sausage
  • 1 onion
  • 1 green bell pepper
  • 3 ribs celery
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1 Tbsp. Cajun seasoning
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lb. red or kidney beans
  • 2 c. water or broth
  • 2 c. cooked white rice

Instructions

  1. Brown the sausage. In a heavy pot over medium-high heat, add sliced sausage in a single layer. Cook for 1-2 minutes per side. Remove and set aside.
  2. Cook the mirepoix. To the same pot, heat a tablespoon of oil and add the chopped onion, green bell pepper, and celery. Saute for 8-10 minutes. Add the garlic and seasonings.
  3. Add the beans and broth. Add in the beans and water or broth. Mix well to combine. Add additional liquid if needed. Cover and reduce heat to a simmer for 60 minutes.
  4. Return sausage. Once beans are tender, return sausage to the pot. Continue to simmer for 5-10 more minutes. Season with additional salt and pepper if desire.
  5. Serve. Ladel a spoon of red beans over cooked white rice. Garnish with green onions if desired. Enjoy!
